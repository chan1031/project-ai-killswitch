Frequently Asked Questions

What are the admission requirements? +

Admission requirements vary by program but generally include high school diploma/transcripts, standardized test scores (SAT/ACT), letters of recommendation, and a personal statement. International students need TOEFL/IELTS scores.

When are the application deadlines? +

For Fall admission: Early Decision (November 15), Regular Decision (January 15). For Spring admission: October 1. Graduate programs have rolling admissions with priority deadlines in December.

What financial aid options are available? +

We offer need-based financial aid, merit scholarships, work-study programs, and student loans. Over 80% of our students receive some form of financial assistance. International students are eligible for merit scholarships.

Can I visit the campus? +

Yes! We offer campus tours Monday through Saturday. Virtual tours are also available. You can schedule a visit through our admissions office or attend one of our open house events held monthly.

What support services are available for students? +

We provide comprehensive support including academic advising, career services, tutoring centers, mental health counseling, disability services, and international student support. Our student-to-advisor ratio is 15:1.
